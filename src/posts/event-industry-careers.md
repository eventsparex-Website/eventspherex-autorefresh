## Freelance vs Full-Time

### Full-Time (Company Employee)

-   **Pros:** Stable income, benefits, training, career growth ladder
-   **Cons:** Fixed salary, limited creative freedom, long hours during events
-   **Best for:** Early career (first 3-5 years) to build skills and network

### Freelance / Independent

-   **Pros:** Higher per-project income, creative freedom, work diversity
-   **Cons:** Irregular income, no benefits, self-marketing required
-   **Best for:** Experienced professionals with a strong network (5+ years)

## Education and Certifications

### Formal Education

-   **MBA in Event Management** — NIEM (Ahmedabad), EMDI Institute, Pearl Academy
-   **Diploma in Event Management** — Available at most major cities
-   **Hotel Management degree** — Strong foundation for hospitality-focused events

### Certifications

-   **CMP (Certified Meeting Professional)** — MPI (global standard)
-   **CSEP (Certified Special Events Professional)** — ILEA
-   **Google Digital Marketing Certification** — Free, valuable for event marketing roles
-   **PMP (Project Management Professional)** — PMI (useful for large event operations)

### Practical Learning

-   Volunteer at events (best way to start)
-   Internships at event companies (3-6 months)
-   Online courses (Coursera, Udemy — event management fundamentals)
-   Industry conferences and networking events

## Career Growth Tips

1\. **Start on the ground** — Work backstage, manage logistics, understand operations 2. **Specialize after 3 years** — Pick a niche (corporate, exhibitions, weddings, tech) 3. **Build your network** — This industry runs on relationships 4. **Document your work** — Portfolio of events you've managed opens doors 5. **Stay updated** — Follow industry publications, attend trade shows 6. **Learn adjacent skills** — Digital marketing, basic design, video editing add immense value 7. **Get comfortable with irregular hours** — Events happen on weekends and late nights

The event industry rewards people who are passionate, adaptable, and willing to hustle. It's not a 9-to-5 career — it's a lifestyle. And for those who love it, there's no better industry.
